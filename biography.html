About me

I currently a Postdoctoral Research Assistant at the Astrophysics Research Institute at Liverpool John Moores University. Since summer 2014 I have been working alongside Dr. Steve Longmore on a project dedicated to understanding massive star and stellar cluster formation towards the Galactic centre. I am particularly interested in the kinematics and dynamics of star forming regions, and how this can help us to understand what is the dominant physics controlling star formation.

I obtained my undergraduate degree (Physics with Astrophysics) from the University of Leeds in 2009, before commencing my Ph. D. (also at the University of Leeds) in 2010. I defended my Ph. D. thesis, entitled "Kinematics and physical structure of a highly filamentary Infrared Dark Cloud" in July 2014. Below is a summary of my curriculum vitae (download a more complete copy here).
